I am working for this Organic-Farm since July 2025 and driving out often at night in the wolf-hour through the fields. After this little village im driving a narrow road through the landscape. The forests sunk into the fog, the grassfields like overgrown piles of boulder, an appletree significant for the long history of the landscape. The regulations for a bio-dynamic agriculture are the sowing days which are follwing the moon and a fertilisation without chemical additives, only Cow-Manure and Clover. Bio/Organic? I trust the term. But others do not, scold with the term Eco-fascism. I also do not trust the Bio-Industry, if e.g. Dennree produce their own products, like the yoghurt, cream, chocolate, pickles – standing next to the products, cheaper than the of the other small farmers or brands. I trust the term more, when farmers don't use chemical additives or genetic engineering, because chemical additives are used in conventional agriculture and i look on signages of wheat, milk or cream.

What is the normative dimension of the expression, to act in accordance with one's nature? Regarding topics such as "organic" products or "100% natural" yogurts, each of us reacted quite quickly in a constructivist—not to say relativistic—way. As soon as someone tells us a product is "natural", it's clear to us that we're being deceived or that a new way has been found to be "artificial".¹
